# Superstore-Sales-Dashboard-
📊 SuperStore Sales Dashboard 

Power BI    An end-to-end Power BI dashboard analyzing retail sales performance, regional trends, customer behavior, shipping patterns, and a 15-day sales forecast — built from a real-world e-commerce dataset spanning 2019–2021.

🎯 Project Objective

The goal of this project was to transform raw SuperStore retail transactional data into a meaningful, interactive Power BI dashboard that business stakeholders can use to:
	•	Monitor overall sales and profitability at a glance
	•	Understand regional and segment performance
	•	Identify top-performing product categories and sub-categories
	•	Analyze shipping behavior and delivery trends
	•	Forecast short-term sales and plan inventory accordingly

🛠️ Tools & Techniques Used

	•	Power BI Desktop — Dashboard design and development
	•	Power Query — Data cleaning and transformation
	•	DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) — KPI measures, calculated columns, and time intelligence
	•	Power BI Analytics Pane — 15-day sales forecasting
	•	Bing Maps Integration — Geographic sales visualization by state
	•	Slicers & Filters — Interactive region and time filtering (Central, East, South, West)

  
  🔍 Key Insights
  
💳 Payment Behavior

	•	COD (Cash on Delivery) is the dominant payment method at 43%, followed by Online at 35% and Cards at 22%
	•	This signals that a large portion of the customer base lacks trust in or access to digital payment options
🌍 Regional Performance

	•	West leads all four regions with 33.37% of total sales (~$534K)
	•	East is a strong second at 28.75% (~$460K)
	•	South significantly underperforms at only 16.1% — the weakest region despite its market size potential
👥 Customer Segments
	•	The Consumer segment drives nearly half of all revenue at 48%
	•	Corporate accounts for 33%, representing a strong but underdeveloped B2B channel
	•	Home Office contributes 19% — a segment that likely grew post-2020 due to remote work trends
📦 Shipping Behavior

	•	Standard Class is overwhelmingly preferred at $330K in sales associated
	•	Same Day delivery accounts for only $30K (3%) — heavily underutilized relative to its potential value
	•	Average ship time across all orders is 4 days — solid, but room exists for premium tier improvement.
	
🗂️ Product Categories

	•	Office Supplies leads at $640K, driven by repeat, high-volume low-ticket purchases
	•	Technology follows at $470K, with Phones alone generating $200K
	•	Furniture rounds out at $450K — highest risk category due to bulkier shipping costs eating into margins.

  📈 Seasonality & Trends
  
	•	Sales and profit both show a clear Q4 spike (November–December), consistent with holiday shopping behavior
	•	February and the mid-year months (May–July) represent the lowest performance windows
	•	Profit closely mirrors sales trends, suggesting relatively stable margins year-round
🗺️ Geographic Distribution

	•	California is the single largest state market at $340K — more than double second-place New York ($190K)
	•	Texas ($120K) and Washington ($90K) represent significant mid-tier opportunities
	•	High concentration in coastal/urban states signals untapped potential in central states
🔮 Sales Forecast (15-Day)

	•	The forecast model projects daily sales between $3,000 – $10,600
	•	High day-to-day volatility (visible in both the spike chart and the zoomed line chart) suggests inconsistent demand patterns
	•	The confidence interval widens in the forecast window, reflecting this unpredictability.

  💡 Business Recommendations
  
1. 💳 Reduce COD Dependency
COD at 43% is operationally expensive — it delays cash flow, increases return risk, and creates logistics complexity.

2. Investigate and Revive the South Region
The South accounts for only 16.1% of sales — a striking underperformance for a large geographic market.

3. 📅 Build a Q4 Sales Strategy
Q4 is clearly the peak season, yet without intentional planning, businesses often miss the full upside.

4. Deepen the Consumer Segment & Grow Corporate
Consumers drive 48% of revenue — but Corporate clients (33%) tend to deliver higher average order values with lower acquisition costs.

5. Expand Mid-Tier State Markets
California and New York dominate, but states like Washington ($90K), Pennsylvania ($80K), Ohio ($70K), and Illinois ($60K) show solid baseline performance with clear upside.
